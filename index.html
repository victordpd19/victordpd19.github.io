Master's Thesis

Development of a Deep Learning Model for Counting and Detecting Animals in Dense Herds from Aerial Images. My thesis extends HerdNet with CBAM attention and Hard Negative Patching to improve multi-species detection and counting in complex aerial environments.

The research demonstrates improved class-level precision and validation robustness, with a particular focus on reducing false positives in occluded and noisy scenes.

About Me

I am a researcher with a master’s background in artificial intelligence, preparing PhD applications for Sweden. My interests include computer vision, aerial animal detection, robotics, and robust deep learning systems for real-world environments.

My master’s thesis extends HerdNet with CBAM attention and Hard Negative Patching to improve detection and counting of animals in dense aerial herds. I also have a bachelor’s project in visual odometry for AGV positioning.
